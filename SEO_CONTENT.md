## What Is Pregnancy & Breastfeeding Procedure Safety Reference?

The Pregnancy & Breastfeeding Procedure Safety Reference is a free, interactive web tool that provides trimester-by-trimester safety guidance for body art procedures. Users select a procedure type and current stage from dropdown menus, and the tool displays a structured assessment with a safety level (safe, caution, or avoid), a summary of the risk rationale, and a list of specific considerations.

The tool evaluates five procedure types, new tattoo, body piercing (non-ear), earlobe piercing, permanent makeup/microblading, and tattoo laser removal, across five client stages: trying to conceive, first trimester, second trimester, third trimester, and breastfeeding/postpartum. Each combination produces a unique result based on documented risks including infection, systemic pigment absorption, anaesthetic use, physical positioning, and healing considerations.

This tool is an educational reference only and explicitly reminds users to consult their midwife, obstetrician, or GP before any body art procedure during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

## Who Should Use This

**Tattoo and piercing studio professionals**, Artists and piercers who need a quick, evidence-informed reference when consulting with clients who are pregnant, breastfeeding, or planning pregnancy. The tool supports informed consent conversations and helps studios maintain responsible practice.

**Permanent makeup artists**, PMU practitioners who need guidance on topical anaesthetic use and pigment safety during pregnancy and breastfeeding stages.

**Pregnant and breastfeeding clients**, Individuals considering body art procedures who want to understand the specific risks at their current stage before consulting their healthcare provider.

**Healthcare providers**, Midwives, obstetricians, and GPs who want a structured reference for discussing body art procedures with pregnant or breastfeeding patients.

**Studio managers and compliance officers**, Professionals developing studio policies around pregnancy and breastfeeding procedures.

## How to Use

**Step 1: Select procedure type**
From the "Procedure type" dropdown, choose the body art procedure you are evaluating. Options include new tattoo, body piercing (non-ear), earlobe piercing, permanent makeup/microblading, or tattoo laser removal.

**Step 2: Select current stage**
From the "Current stage" dropdown, select the client's stage: trying to conceive, first trimester (weeks 1–12), second trimester (weeks 13–26), third trimester (weeks 27–40), or breastfeeding/postpartum.

**Step 3: Review the safety assessment**
The tool displays a result card with:
- A safety banner showing the level (safe, caution, or avoid) with an icon and brief label
- A summary explaining the risk rationale
- A list of specific considerations for that procedure-stage combination
- A reminder to discuss with a healthcare provider

**Step 4: Change selections as needed**
Adjust either dropdown to see different procedure-stage combinations. Results update instantly without page reload.

## Use Case Examples

**Use Case 1: Piercing artist consulting with pregnant client**

A piercing artist has a client in her second trimester requesting a navel piercing. The artist selects "Body piercing (non-ear)" and "Second trimester (weeks 13–26)". The tool returns a "Use caution, avoid navel" result, explaining that navel piercings will migrate or reject as the abdomen expands. The specific considerations list includes blood vessel dilation increasing bleeding risk and hormonal changes affecting healing. The artist uses this information to recommend against the navel piercing and discuss alternative placements.

**Use Case 2: Client considering tattoo while breastfeeding**

A new mother wants a tattoo while breastfeeding. She selects "New tattoo" and "Breastfeeding / postpartum". The tool returns "Use caution" with a summary explaining that no established evidence shows tattoo inks transfer meaningfully to breast milk, but data is limited. The specific considerations note infection risk during nursing and potential effects on milk supply. The client takes this information to her GP for a personalised discussion.

**Use Case 3: PMU artist evaluating first-trimester client**

A permanent makeup artist has a client who is 8 weeks pregnant and wants microblading. The artist selects "Permanent makeup / microblading" and "First trimester (weeks 1–12)". The tool returns "Avoid" with a summary explaining that topical anaesthetics and dermal pigments are unstudied for fetal safety during organogenesis. The specific considerations list topical anaesthetic contraindications and unpredictable immune response. The artist uses this to respectfully decline the procedure and suggest revisiting after breastfeeding.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q1: Is this tool a substitute for medical advice?**
No. This tool is an educational reference only. Always consult your midwife, obstetrician, or GP before any body art procedure during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

**Q2: What procedures does this tool cover?**
The tool covers five procedure types: new tattoo, body piercing (non-ear), earlobe piercing, permanent makeup/microblading, and tattoo laser removal.

**Q3: What stages does the tool evaluate?**
The tool evaluates five stages: trying to conceive, first trimester (weeks 1–12), second trimester (weeks 13–26), third trimester (weeks 27–40), and breastfeeding/postpartum.

**Q4: How are safety levels determined?**
Each procedure-stage combination is assigned one of three levels: safe (low risk with standard precautions), caution (moderate risk requiring careful consideration), or avoid (not recommended due to significant risk).

**Q5: Does the tool cover nipple piercings specifically?**
The tool includes a specific warning for nipple piercings during breastfeeding, advising against them due to infection risk and potential interference with milk ducts. For other stages, nipple piercings fall under the general body piercing guidance.

**Q6: Why does the tool recommend avoiding navel piercings in the second trimester?**
Abdominal expansion during pregnancy causes navel piercings to migrate or reject. The tool explicitly advises against navel piercings during the second trimester regardless of the general caution level for other piercings.

**Q7: Does the tool address tattoo ink safety during pregnancy?**
Yes. The tool notes that ink pigments can enter lymphatic tissue and systemic circulation, and that data on fetal effects is limited. It recommends EU Regulation 2020/2081 compliant inks where available.

**Q8: Can I use this tool for a client who is trying to conceive?**
Yes. The tool includes a "Trying to conceive" stage for all five procedure types, providing guidance on risks during the conception window.

**Q9: Does the tool cover laser tattoo removal during breastfeeding?**
Yes. The tool advises against laser tattoo removal during breastfeeding, noting that fragmented pigment metabolites may potentially transfer to breast milk and that most laser clinics recommend waiting until nursing is complete.

**Q10: How often is the tool updated?**
The tool is based on current evidence and professional guidelines. Always verify with your healthcare provider, as individual circumstances may change the risk assessment significantly.
